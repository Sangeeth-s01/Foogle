Snacks & Appentizers

Aloo Tikki (Potato Patties)

Ingredients:

3 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed 1/2 cup green peas, boiled and mashed 1 tablespoon ginger, grated 1-2 green chilies, chopped 1 teaspoon cumin powder 1 teaspoon garam masala Salt to taste Oil for shallow frying Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (optional)

Instructions:

Prepare the Mixture: In a large bowl, combine mashed potatoes, mashed peas, ginger, green chilies, cumin powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well. You can add chopped coriander leaves for extra flavor. Shape the Tikkis: Divide the mixture into equal portions. Roll each portion into a ball and flatten it slightly to form a patty (tikki). You can make them as thick or thin as you prefer. Heat the Oil: In a pan, heat oil on medium heat. Make sure the oil is hot enough before frying the tikkis to prevent them from absorbing too much oil. Fry the Tikkis: Carefully place the tikkis in the hot oil. Fry them in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. Cook until they are golden brown and crispy on both sides (about 3-4 minutes per side). Serve: Drain the tikkis on paper towels to remove excess oil. Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.


Vegetable Samosas

Ingredients:

For the Dough: 2 cups all-purpose flour 1/4 cup oil or ghee 1/2 teaspoon salt Water (as needed) For the Filling: 2 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed 1/2 cup green peas, boiled 1 teaspoon cumin seeds 1 teaspoon garam masala 1 teaspoon chili powder 1 teaspoon coriander powder Salt to taste Oil for frying

Instructions:

Make the Dough: In a bowl, mix flour, salt, and oil. Gradually add water and knead to form a smooth dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes. Prepare the Filling: Heat a little oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter. Add the mashed potatoes, peas, garam masala, chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. Let it cool. Shape the Samosas: Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a circle and cut it in half. Take one half, form a cone, and seal the edge with water. Fill the cone with the vegetable filling and seal the top edge. Fry the Samosas: Heat oil in a deep pan for frying. Once hot, carefully add the samosas in batches. Fry until they turn golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Serve: Drain on paper towels and serve hot with mint or tamarind chutney.


Paneer Pakora (Paneer Fritters)

Ingredients:

200g paneer, sliced into cubes 1 cup besan (gram flour) 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder 1/2 teaspoon ajwain (carom seeds) Salt to taste Water (as needed) Oil for frying

Instructions:

Prepare the Batter: In a bowl, mix besan, turmeric powder, red chili powder, ajwain, and salt. Gradually add water to make a thick batter. Heat Oil: In a deep pan, heat oil over medium heat for frying. Coat the Paneer: Dip each paneer cube into the batter, ensuring it is fully coated. Fry the Pakoras: Carefully drop the batter-coated paneer into the hot oil. Fry in batches until golden brown and crispy (about 3-4 minutes). Serve: Remove pakoras from the oil and drain on paper towels. Serve hot with green chutney or ketchup.

Bhel Puri (Puffed Rice Snack)

Ingredients:

2 cups puffed rice 1/2 cup boiled potatoes, diced 1/2 cup chopped onions 1/2 cup chopped tomatoes 1/4 cup boiled peas 1/4 cup sev (crispy chickpea noodles) 2 tablespoons tamarind chutney 2 tablespoons green chutney Salt, chaat masala, and lemon juice to taste Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions:

Prepare the Mixture: In a large mixing bowl, combine puffed rice, boiled potatoes, chopped onions, chopped tomatoes, and boiled peas. Add Chutneys and Spices: Add tamarind chutney, green chutney, salt, chaat masala, and lemon juice to the mixture. Mix everything well. Add Sev: Just before serving, add sev to the mixture for crunch. Garnish: Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
